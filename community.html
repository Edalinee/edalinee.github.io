Volunteering History

Volunteering is an amazing chance for me to get engaged in my community, meet new people and meet my desire of helping others

Hack UTD

In early 2016, I was provided a chance to help in the set-up of a 24-hour hackathon in my local area. A Hackathon is an event that provides periods of building and designing leisure code for the 500 of University of Texas at Dallas students it hosts, and encompasses a variety of prizes, competitions and games. In my time there I served meals to the patrons, directed students to their designated areas, and disposed of excess waste. In addition to the casual civil duties, I got to engage in the life of college students and experienced coders. I was able to observe their style of coding and learn new aspects of programming.

Lake Rockwall Estates

Beginning in the fall of 2015, Every Tuesday I volunteer at the Lake Rockwall Estates Community Center. The kids there range from age 5-16. There I aide in the tutoring of the children and help with their homework. The center has a large Hispanic population of youngsters which means that most of the children speak Spanish as their first language and that some of their homework was also in Spanish. My background in the Spanish language from high school made me the only volunteer that could help the children on their homework in Spanish and relate to the kids on a different level. Being around a plethora of minors also provided me with the skills to resolve conflicts, instill verbal discipline, and create connections with the adolescents. I enjoy the moments I spend connecting with each one of the children every time I walk through the door
